Jollof Rice

Parboiled long grain rice, tomatoes, red bell pepper (tatashe), scotch bonnet, onion, tomato paste, chicken or beef stock, vegetable oil, bay leaves, curry, thyme, seasoning cubes, salt.

Ingredients

  • Parboiled Long Grain Rice(3cups)
  • Tomatoes(4)
  • Two Red Bell Peppers(tatashe)
  • Two Scotch Bonnet Peppers
  • Two Onions
  • Two tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken/beef stock
  • 0.3 cups vegetable oil
  • two bay leaves
  • one teaspoon curry powder
  • one teaspoon thyme
  • two seasoning cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Blend the base: Blend 4 tomatoes, 2 red bell pepper (tatashe), 2 scotch bonnet peppers, and one of 2 onions together until smooth.
  2. Fry tomato paste: Heat 0.3 cups vegetable oil in a pot, add the remaining 2 onions (sliced) and fry for 5 minutes Add 2 tablespoons tomato paste and fry for another 3 minutes until the paste darkens slightly
  3. Cook down the pepper: Pour in blended pepper. Fry on medium-high heat for 15–20 minutes, stirring often, until the raw smell is gone and the oil floats to the top.
  4. Season the sauce: Add 2 cups chicken or beef stock, 2 bay leaves, 1 teaspoons curry powder, 1 teaspoons thyme, 2 seasoning cubes, and 1 teaspoons salt. Stir and bring to a boil.
  5. Add rice: Wash and add 3 cups parboiled long grain rice. Stir once to combine, reduce heat to low, and cover tightly with foil then the lid.
  6. Cook until done: Cook for 25–30 minutes on low heat. Check halfway — if dry, add a splash of stock. For party jollof, turn heat to the lowest setting for the last 5 minutes and leave the lid on.
