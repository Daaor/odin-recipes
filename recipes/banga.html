Banga Soup

Ingredients

  • 500 grams fresh or canned palm fruits
  • 500 grams assorted meat (beef, goat, offal)
  • 150 grams dried fish
  • 3 tablespoons crayfish
  • 1 oburunbebe stick
  • 2 tablespoons beletete leaves
  • 1 onion
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 teaspoons salt
  • 200 grams periwinkle (optional)

Steps

  1. Extract palm fruit: If using fresh 500 grams fresh or canned palm fruits, boil for 20 minutes until soft. Pound in a mortar, add warm water, and squeeze out the palm extract. Strain out the fibres and seeds. If using canned, open and use directly.
  2. Cook the meat: Season 500 grams assorted meat (beef, goat, offal) with 1 onion (chopped), 2 seasoning cubes, and 1 teaspoons salt. Boil until tender. Set aside with the stock.
  3. Boil the extract: Pour palm fruit extract into a pot and bring to a boil. Add the 1 oburunbebe stick and let it simmer in the extract for 10 minutes to release its flavour.
  4. Add meat, fish and pepper: Add cooked 500 grams assorted meat (beef, goat, offal), 150 grams dried fish, 3 tablespoons crayfish, 2 scotch bonnet peppers, and meat stock. Stir and cook for 15 minutes on medium heat.
  5. Finish and serve: Add 2 tablespoons beletete leaves and 200 grams periwinkle (optional) if using. Taste and adjust seasoning. Cook for another 5 minutes. Remove the oburunbebe stick before serving.
